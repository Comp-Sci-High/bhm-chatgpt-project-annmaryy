Learn More About These Powerful Women

Angela Davis

Angela Davis is a political activist, scholar, and author known for her work in civil rights, prison abolition, and social justice. She was a leader in the Communist Party USA and had close ties to the Black Panther Party. Davis gained international attention when she was accused of being involved in a prison break attempt but was later acquitted. She has since become an advocate for prison reform and has written several books on racial justice and feminism.

Sojourner Truth

Sojourner Truth was a former enslaved woman who became a powerful abolitionist and women’s rights activist. She is best known for her speech “Ain’t I a Woman?” which she delivered in 1851 at the Women’s Convention in Ohio. Truth worked tirelessly to end slavery and fought for equal rights for all, including women’s suffrage. Her activism took her across the country, where she gave speeches and helped freed slaves find new homes.

Rosa Parks

Rosa Parks is known as the “Mother of the Civil Rights Movement” for her courageous act of refusing to give up her seat to a white man on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama, in 1955. Her arrest led to the Montgomery Bus Boycott, which lasted over a year and helped bring national attention to segregation in the South. Parks continued to fight for civil rights throughout her life, working alongside leaders such as Martin Luther King Jr.
